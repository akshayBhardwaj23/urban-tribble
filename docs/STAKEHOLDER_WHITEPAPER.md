# Snaptix Stakeholder White Paper

## Executive Summary

Snaptix is an AI-powered decision layer for spreadsheet-first businesses. It helps teams turn Excel and CSV files into dashboards, executive briefings, forecasts, and plain-English answers without the setup cost or complexity of traditional BI workflows.

The opportunity is clear: most businesses already have data, but they do not have fast, consistent, decision-ready insight. Reporting is fragmented, manual, and slow. Snaptix closes that gap by transforming operational spreadsheets into an intelligent workspace that compounds context over time.

This paper outlines the problem, the product, the target users, the market need, the business model, and why Snaptix is positioned to become a valuable operating layer for modern teams.

---

## 1. The Problem

Businesses do not fail because they lack data. They fail because they lack clarity at the moment decisions need to be made.

Across small businesses, scaling teams, and even large enterprises, the same pattern appears:

- Important business information lives in Excel sheets, CSV exports, and disconnected reports.
- Teams spend hours or days cleaning files, aligning definitions, and preparing reviews.
- Decision-makers often receive summaries too late to act meaningfully.
- Existing BI tools can be powerful, but they are often too slow, too technical, or too heavyweight for day-to-day operating decisions.
- Generic AI tools can explain data, but they do not provide continuity, reliable grounding, or a persistent business context.

The result is delayed action, duplicated effort, and decision-making based on partial understanding.

---

## 2. The Shift in the Market

There is a growing gap between the amount of data businesses generate and their ability to operationalize it quickly.

Three realities define this shift:

1. Spreadsheets are still the default operating system for many teams.
2. Leaders increasingly expect AI to reduce reporting overhead, not just generate text.
3. Business users want clarity without waiting on a central analytics function.

This creates a strong market need for a product that is:

- spreadsheet-native,
- easy for non-technical users,
- useful in minutes,
- and credible enough for recurring operational review.

Snaptix is built for that exact gap.

---

## 3. What Snaptix Is

Snaptix is the AI business analyst for spreadsheet-first teams.

It allows users to:

- upload Excel and CSV files,
- auto-generate dashboards and KPIs,
- receive executive-style AI briefings,
- compare what changed across time,
- forecast future performance,
- and ask questions in plain English against real data.

Rather than acting like a generic chatbot, Snaptix operates as a structured business workflow:

**upload -> clean -> analyze -> brief -> question -> decide**

The product is not only about insights. It is about reducing the time between data arrival and action.

---

## 4. How the Product Works

At a high level, Snaptix converts raw spreadsheet inputs into a reusable decision workspace.

### Workflow

1. A user uploads Excel or CSV files.
2. Snaptix cleans and structures the data automatically.
3. The system detects useful fields such as date, revenue-like columns, categories, and metrics.
4. Dashboards and KPIs are generated instantly.
5. AI produces a briefing that explains what changed, why it matters, and what to do next.
6. Users can ask follow-up questions in natural language.
7. As more files are added over time, the workspace becomes a compounding history of the business.

### Why this matters

This turns a static reporting task into a living operating system for review, monitoring, and action.

---

## 5. Who Uses Snaptix

Snaptix is best understood through the people who use it, not just company size.

### 5.1 Small business owners and founders

These users often do not have a dedicated analyst. They need something easy, fast, and understandable.

Snaptix helps them:

- understand business performance without learning BI tools,
- get plain-English summaries instead of only charts,
- identify what changed and what action is needed,
- and make decisions without relying on manual spreadsheet interpretation.

### 5.2 Operations, finance, and RevOps leads

These users manage recurring reviews and internal reporting with limited time and limited resources.

Snaptix helps them:

- unify files across teams and periods,
- reduce manual review preparation,
- create repeatable monthly or weekly business reviews,
- and align teams around one source of operational truth.

### 5.3 Analysts

These users may already know Excel, SQL, Power BI, or Tableau. For them, Snaptix is not a replacement for every analytics workflow. It is a speed layer.

Snaptix helps them:

- move faster on spreadsheet-heavy work,
- answer ad hoc business questions quickly,
- share stakeholder-friendly summaries,
- and complement, rather than replace, more advanced BI systems.

### 5.4 Enterprise operators

In larger organizations, many important teams still run on exports and spreadsheets, even when warehouses and dashboards exist.

Snaptix helps them:

- get fast answers without waiting on dashboard backlogs,
- support non-technical operators,
- and provide an agile decision layer on top of existing systems.

## 6. Why Snaptix Wins

Snaptix occupies a practical middle ground between spreadsheets, BI suites, and generic AI tools.

### Compared with spreadsheets alone

- faster insight,
- less manual cleanup,
- more consistent interpretation,
- and reusable history across time.

### Compared with traditional BI tools

- faster time-to-value,
- easier for non-technical users,
- lower setup friction,
- and more natural for spreadsheet-first workflows.

### Compared with generic AI tools

- persistent workspace memory,
- answers grounded in real uploaded data,
- structured business briefings,
- and recurring analytical continuity instead of one-off chat sessions.

Snaptix is not trying to replace every enterprise analytics system. It is designed to become the fastest path from spreadsheet to decision.

---

## 7. Product Differentiators

Snaptix stands out on five dimensions:

### 7.1 Spreadsheet-native from day one

Users do not need a data warehouse migration to receive value.

### 7.2 Grounded AI

The product is built around real uploaded files, not abstract prompts alone.

### 7.3 Decision-oriented output

The system does not stop at chart generation. It is built to tell users what changed, why it matters, and what deserves attention next.

### 7.4 Continuity over time

The workspace becomes more valuable with repeated use because uploads, comparisons, briefings, and monitoring accumulate context.

### 7.5 Accessible to both technical and non-technical users

Small business owners can use it without BI expertise, while analysts can use it as a fast operational layer.

## 8. Business Model

Snaptix uses a tiered subscription model with clear progression:

- **Free** for initial product trial and first business review,
- **Starter** for recurring monthly review workflows,
- **Pro** for continuous monitoring, deeper insight, alerts, history, and multi-workspace usage.

This model aligns pricing with business maturity:

- smaller teams can start with low friction,
- growing teams can adopt recurring workflows,
- and more advanced teams can expand usage as reporting needs scale.

The monetization logic is strong because the product is tied directly to decision quality, time savings, and recurring review cycles.

## 9. Why Stakeholders Should Care

Snaptix is not merely a reporting interface. It is a strategic productivity layer for business decision-making.

For stakeholders, the relevance is substantial:

- It addresses a widespread and durable workflow: spreadsheet-based reporting.
- It uses AI in a concrete, operationally valuable way.
- It lowers the barrier between raw business data and executive action.
- It supports expansion from individual users to team-based recurring workflows.
- It can coexist with existing tools rather than requiring a disruptive replacement motion.

In short, it is easier to adopt than enterprise BI, more useful than generic AI, and more scalable than manual spreadsheet reviews.

## 10. Strategic Vision

The long-term vision for Snaptix is to become the decision layer for spreadsheet-first businesses.

That means evolving from:

- file-level insight,
- to workspace-level intelligence,
- to historical context,
- to proactive monitoring,
- and eventually to an embedded operating system for weekly and monthly business reviews.

The product’s compounding advantage comes from repeated use:

- more uploads create more context,
- more context improves briefings,
- better briefings increase trust,
- and higher trust increases recurring use.

This creates the conditions for durable retention and deeper workflow ownership.

Snaptix is built around a simple but powerful truth:
